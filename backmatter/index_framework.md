## Concepts

**A* search**, 5, 7, B --- evaluation function $f(x) = g(x) + h(x)$; System 2 + System 1; economic pathfinding on the decision complex
**admissible heuristic**, 5, 10 --- $h(v) \leq h^*(v)$; admissible price = efficient market; inadmissible = market failure
**attribute conservation**, 9 --- Noether's theorem applied to bilateral exchange; value not created by relabeling
**attribute vector** ($\mathbf{a} \in \mathbb{R}^9$), 3, 4, 7 --- point on the decision manifold; nine economic dimensions
**augmented game** ($\Gamma^+$), 7 --- normal-form game whose Nash equilibria are exactly the BGE
**autonomy** ($d_4$), 3, 4, 7 --- freedom of economic action; transferable dimension

**behavioral friction** (BF), 5, 7 --- total path cost on the manifold; quantity minimized at BGE
**Bond geodesic** ($\gamma^*$), 7 --- minimum-cost path on the economic decision complex
**Bond Geodesic Equilibrium** (BGE), 7, 10, 13, 16 --- multi-agent equilibrium on the full manifold; no agent can reduce BF by unilateral deviation; Nash is scalar projection (Theorem 21)
**Bond Invariance Principle** (BIP), 8, 9 --- economic evaluations must be invariant under meaning-preserving transformations
**boundary penalty** ($\beta_k$), 3, 4, 7 --- discrete cost of crossing a moral or institutional constraint
**bubble, geometric**, 11 --- dimensional divergence: $g_{11}$ contracts while $g_{55}$, $g_{99}$ expand

**comparative advantage, geometric**, 13 --- nations specialize where their manifold is flattest; curvature statement
**consequences** ($d_1$), 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 --- monetary payoff dimension; the dimension captured by classical economics
**conservation of value**, 9 --- Noether's theorem: continuous scaling symmetry implies value cannot be created by relabeling
**contraction lemma**, 7 --- BGE is unique when $\alpha_i > \kappa_i$ for all agents (weak coupling)
**cost-benefit analysis**, 1, 6 --- scalar projection of the economic tensor; subject to irrecoverability
**covariance matrix** ($\Sigma$), 4, 11, C --- $9 \times 9$ matrix encoding dimension interactions; determines the metric
**cross-sensitivity** ($\kappa_i$), 7 --- how much agent $i$'s behavioral friction depends on others' strategies
**curvature, sectional** ($K$), 11 --- diverges at financial crisis singularities; small perturbation $\to$ large response
**curvature singularity**, 11 --- *see* financial crisis as singularity

**denomination effect**, 8 --- gauge violation: economic evaluation changes with denomination
**dictator game**, 7 --- BGE explains non-zero offers via $d_3$ (fairness) and $d_7$ (identity)
**dimensional divergence**, 11 --- *see* bubble, geometric
**discount rate**, 15 --- geodesic curvature on the intertemporal manifold; Stern-Nordhaus debate as curvature disagreement

**economic decision complex** ($\mathcal{E}$), 3, 4, 5, 7 --- weighted simplicial complex of economic states and transitions
**economic gauge group** ($\mathcal{G} = \mathbb{R}^+ \times S_n$), 8, 9 --- scaling (currency, units) $\times$ relabeling (numeraire choice)
**economic metric**, 4 --- Mahalanobis distance + boundary penalties; determined by $\Sigma^{-1}$
**economic tensor** ($T^{\mu}{}_{\nu}$), 6 --- obligations (vectors) contracted with interests (covectors)
**efficient market hypothesis**, 5 --- equivalent to admissibility of the price heuristic
**endowment effect**, 4, 7 --- predicted WTA/WTP ratio of 1.66 via Finsler metric asymmetry
**epistemic quality** ($d_9$), 3, 11 --- information quality dimension; degrades in bubbles and crises
**evaluative dimensions** ($d_5$--$d_9$), 3 --- trust, social impact, identity, legitimacy, epistemic; not directly transferable
**externality**, 10 --- heuristic corruption: price signal does not encode social cost

**fairness** ($d_3$), 3, 4, 7, 12 --- distributive justice dimension; key to ultimatum game predictions
**failure modes, four**, 10 --- heuristic corruption, objective hijacking, local minima, gauge breaking
**financial crisis as singularity**, 11 --- $\det(\Sigma) \to 0$; metric degeneracy; geodesic incompleteness
**Finsler metric**, 4, A --- asymmetric distance; loss aversion as metric asymmetry

**gauge breaking**, 8, 10 --- money illusion, denomination effects, sunk cost fallacy
**gauge group**, *see* economic gauge group
**gauge invariance**, 8, 9 --- economic evaluations invariant under scaling and relabeling
**gauge violation tensor** ($V_{ij}$), 8 --- measures failure of gauge invariance across transformations and dimensions
**GDP as tensor contraction**, 6 --- $\text{tr}(T) = T^{\mu}{}_{\mu}$; a specific contraction discarding 8 of 9 dimensions
**geodesic incompleteness**, 11 --- at singularity, no geodesic connects current state to goal; pathfinding fails

**heuristic corruption**, 5, 10 --- price signal overestimates or underestimates true cost; information asymmetry, externalities
**heuristic field**, 5 --- the price signal as a scalar field guiding economic A* search

**identity / virtue** ($d_7$), 3, 7 --- self-conception dimension; active in ultimatum game rejection
**inequality as metric distortion**, 12 --- different agents experience different metrics; a dollar has different curvature for rich and poor
**invisible hand**, 2 --- Adam Smith's insight reinterpreted as gradient flow on the economic manifold

**legitimacy** ($d_8$), 3, 8 --- institutional and regulatory dimension; regulatory goodwill
**local minima**, 10, 11 --- poverty traps, bubbles; locally stable but globally suboptimal states
**loss aversion** ($\lambda$), 4 --- Finsler asymmetry; varies 1.0--3.53 with dimensional activation (contradicts constant $\lambda$)

**Mahalanobis distance**, 4, A --- $\sqrt{(x-y)^T \Sigma^{-1} (x-y)}$; natural metric on the economic manifold
**market failure**, 5, 10 --- geometric pathology on the decision complex; four types
**metric degeneracy**, 11 --- $\det(\Sigma) \to 0$; at crisis singularity; distances become infinite
**metric regularization**, 11 --- crisis recovery: restoring $\Sigma$ to positive definiteness; QE, stress tests, emergency lending
**mixed BGE**, 7 --- probability distribution over paths; existence guaranteed by Nash's theorem
**money illusion**, 8 --- gauge violation: evaluation changes with nominal units despite constant real value

**Nash equilibrium**, 7, A --- scalar projection of BGE; the special case with $\Sigma = \sigma_1^2 \mathbf{e}_1 \mathbf{e}_1^T$ and $\beta_k = 0$
**Noether's theorem**, 9, A --- continuous symmetry $\to$ conservation law; applied to economic gauge group
**numeraire**, 8 --- the good used as measuring unit; $S_n$ permutes numeraire choice

**objective hijacking**, 10 --- agent optimizes proxy (quarterly earnings, stock price) instead of true objective (firm value)

**poverty trap**, 10, 12 --- local minimum on the manifold; locally stable but globally suboptimal
**price heuristic** ($h(x)$), 5, 10 --- price signal as scalar field; admissible = efficient
**prospect theory**, 4, 7, C --- Kahneman-Tversky anomalies predicted by Finsler asymmetry and dimensional activation
**public goods game**, 7, C --- predicted at 4.3% out-of-sample error via $d_6$ (social impact) activation

**regulation as boundary enforcement**, 14 --- environmental, financial regulation as forbidden regions or curvature constraints on the manifold
**rights** ($d_2$), 3, 4 --- property and contract rights dimension; transferable

**Scalar Irrecoverability Theorem**, 6, A --- no continuous $\phi: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ ($n > 1$) is injective; GDP destroys 8 dimensions irrecoverably
**scalar projection** ($\pi_{d_1}$), 6, 7, 11 --- the map extracting the monetary component; what classical economics computes
**self-separation** ($\alpha_i$), 7 --- how much agent $i$'s own costs depend on their own path choice
**simplicial complex**, 3, A --- the underlying combinatorial structure of the economic decision complex
**social impact** ($d_6$), 3, 10, 14 --- externalities and community dimension; key in public goods games
**sunk cost fallacy**, 8 --- gauge violation: path-dependent evaluation violating re-description invariance

**trade as geodesic exchange**, 13 --- trade restrictions as artificial boundaries; comparative advantage as curvature
**transferable dimensions** ($d_1$--$d_4$), 3 --- consequences, rights, fairness, autonomy; can be directly exchanged
**trust** ($d_5$), 3, 7, 11 --- relational and institutional trust; collapses in financial crises ($g_{55} \to \infty$)

**ultimatum game**, 7, C --- BGE predicts rejection of unfair offers via $d_3$, $d_7$ activation; cross-cultural variation via metric differences

**value conservation**, *see* conservation of value

## Mathematical Objects and Notation

**$\mathbf{a} \in \mathbb{R}^9$** (attribute vector), 3, 4 --- point on the decision manifold
**$\beta_k$** (boundary penalty), 4, 7 --- discrete cost of crossing constraint $k$
**BF** (behavioral friction), 5, 7 --- total path cost; $\sum w(v_i, v_{i+1})$
**BGE** (Bond Geodesic Equilibrium), 7 --- $\gamma_i^* = \arg\min_{\gamma_i} \text{BF}_i(\gamma_i \mid \gamma_{-i}^*)$
**$\mathcal{E}$** (economic decision complex), 3 --- weighted simplicial complex
**$f(x) = g(x) + h(x)$** (A* evaluation), 5 --- System 2 cost + System 1 heuristic
**$\mathcal{G} = \mathbb{R}^+ \times S_n$** (gauge group), 8 --- scaling $\times$ numeraire relabeling
**$\gamma^*$** (Bond geodesic), 7 --- minimum-cost path on $\mathcal{E}$
**$\Gamma^+$** (augmented game), 7 --- Nash game equivalent to BGE
**$g_{\mu\nu} = (\Sigma^{-1})_{\mu\nu}$** (metric tensor), 4, 11 --- determines distances on the manifold
**$h(x)$** (price heuristic), 5 --- scalar field estimating cost-to-go
**$K(u,v)$** (sectional curvature), 11 --- $R(u,v,v,u) / [g(u,u)g(v,v) - g(u,v)^2]$
**$\kappa_i$** (cross-sensitivity), 7 --- sensitivity of agent $i$'s cost to others' strategies
**$\alpha_i$** (self-separation), 7 --- sensitivity of agent $i$'s cost to own strategy
**$L = \max_i(\kappa_i/\alpha_i)$** (Lipschitz constant), 7 --- $L < 1$ implies unique BGE
**$\lambda$** (loss aversion), 4 --- Finsler asymmetry coefficient; varies 1.0--3.53
**$\Sigma$** (covariance matrix), 4, 11, C --- $9 \times 9$ SPD matrix; $\det(\Sigma) \to 0$ at singularity
**$T^{\mu}{}_{\nu}$** (economic tensor), 6 --- obligations contracted with interests; GDP = $\text{tr}(T)$
**$V_{ij}$** (gauge violation tensor), 8 --- measures invariance failure by transformation and dimension
**$w(v_i, v_j)$** (edge weight), 4 --- $\Delta\mathbf{a}^T \Sigma^{-1} \Delta\mathbf{a} + \sum_k \beta_k$
