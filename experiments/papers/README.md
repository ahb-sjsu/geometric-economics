# Geometric Decision Theory (GDT) — a body of work

A foundation, not a series. One central claim — the theory of **Geometric Decision Theory (GDT)** —
stated as mathematics and discharged by empirics, each piece written to stand alone and held to exactly
the confidence its evidence supports. The ambition is in the *sharpness of the claim* and the *rigor of
the apparatus* — never in inflated results. ("The geometry" / "the geometric model" are used informally
for GDT throughout.)

## The central claim

> Choice is navigation on a low-dimensional decision manifold. Scalar utility is an information-losing
> projection of it. One shared metric governs risky, strategic, and social choice, and there exist
> regimes where that metric predicts behavior no scalar theory can express — signed, out of sample, and
> invariant to how the researcher encodes the attributes.

## The load-bearing honesty (what makes a foundation)

Each piece names what it does **not** establish:

- Paper 1 ties CPT on lotteries (does not beat it) and transfers only the *risk* part of the metric,
  not the social coordinates; level-k does not help.
- Paper 2 is a **model-organism** result (language models, not humans); two pre-registered predictions
  failed (loss reversal; dose shape) and are reported, not buried; the failures localized to the
  temperature, which was redesigned and re-registered.
- Paper 0's nesting theorems are necessary and therefore **scientifically empty on their own**; the
  content is entirely in the two empirical obligations.

A foundation is only as strong as the honesty of its weakest claim. Every prediction here was frozen
and hashed before data (`prereg-v1` scored; `prereg-v2` for the human study); every competitor was
judged under a common protocol; every dead end is on the record.

## The path to the decisive result

The program is *pointed* at one experiment: the **human projection-gap** under the redesigned rule,
powered by Paper 2's effect sizes (d ≈ 0.5–0.9), with encoding-invariant signs and pre-committed
falsifiers. That result — not any paper here — is what would move the work from "a rigorous, honest,
field-relevant reframing of decision theory" toward the kind of decisive, adopted phenomenon that
reshapes a field. The papers are the solid ground it stands on.
